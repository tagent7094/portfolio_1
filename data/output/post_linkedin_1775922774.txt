I cannot be the only one who feels like history is repeating itself. I just spent 20 mins scrolling LinkedIn. Everyone has some AI product that they want to showcase.  

Meanwhile, I'm remembering that call center at 19. They handed me the alias "Nathan" and told me to change my name and my accent while studying engineering. The promise was cost savings. The reality was identity erasure.  

Stop calling it AI augmentation. It's the same script. Automation replacing humans. Offshoring replacing local talent. Different year. Same Cobra Effect. The tools change but the perverse incentives stay locked in.  

It's not about replacing humans. It's about exposing who was actually adding value. That's what AI does. It reveals the truth that offshoring tried to hide - that authenticity creates value, not cost-cutting.  

You know what that required? The courage to keep showing up as myself. Not Nathan. Not the accent they wanted. The voice that fought back against being erased.  

What name is your AI product forcing you to become?
