The CEO onstage said 'AI-first' three times in ten minutes. His CTO was on his phone.

Here's what people miss about the "use more AI" mandate. It's not a strategy. It's a tell.

If your CEO keeps saying it but isn't personally building tools, testing workflows, or sitting with customers watching the technology change their day, that company probably won't exist in five years.

I closed our first $2M before handing that work to anyone else. Not because I didn't trust the team. Because you can't ask someone to figure out what you haven't figured out yourself. AI is no different.

Meanwhile, late-stage founders are delegating the entire AI transformation to a product lead or a task force. Issuing orders from a map they've never walked.

It's not about management anymore. It's about whether you've touched the thing you're telling everyone else to touch.

The founders who experiment and break things themselves will shape what comes next. The rest will keep writing roadmaps for a world that's already gone.

Not wrong. Just late.
