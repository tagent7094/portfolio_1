Our CTO sent a company-wide AI mandate, then Slacked me asking how prompts work.

If your CEO keeps telling everyone to "use more AI" but hasn't personally built a single workflow with it, that's not leadership. It's delegation dressed up as vision.

I've watched this pattern before. Executives who mandated offshoring to cut costs but never sat on a customer call themselves. The numbers looked great on a slide deck. Meanwhile, 85% of agents were getting rejected for their accents and customer satisfaction collapsed underneath them. The directive created the exact problem it was meant to solve.

AI adoption is heading down the same road. A founder who won't get their hands dirty with the technology can't know what it changes, what it breaks, or where the real opportunity sits. It's not about managing a transformation. It's about whether you understand the tool well enough to see what your team can't.

The companies that survive the next five years won't be run by people who announce AI strategies in all-hands meetings. They'll be run by founders who broke something this week, rebuilt it, and shipped it before anyone wrote a memo about it.

Meanwhile, next quarter's roadmap review is already on the calendar. No customers in the room. There never were.
