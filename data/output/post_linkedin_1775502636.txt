Halfway through our 'AI-first' memo, I realized I hadn't touched the product in nine days.

I've watched founders send company-wide emails about "AI-first culture" but can't show you a single workflow they've built themselves.

Their teams notice. Everybody notices.

It's not about management anymore. It's about whether you've actually touched the thing you're telling everyone else to use.

I built our first demos myself. Sat on customer calls. Broke things. Fixed them. Broke them again. You know what that required? Not a strategy deck. Just the willingness to look stupid for a few weeks.

Meanwhile, the founders who outsource their understanding to a "Head of AI" are doing the same thing founders do when they hire a VP of Sales before earning their first dollar. Delegating conviction.

And you can't delegate conviction.

Stop telling your team to adopt AI. Start building something with it yourself, even badly, and watch how fast the culture follows.
