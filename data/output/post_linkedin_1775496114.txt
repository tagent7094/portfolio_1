The hype telling him otherwise isn't just inaccurate. It's stealing ambition from people who haven't even had a chance to build yet.

One of my teams actually built a decent AI resume screening assistant. You know what that required? Months of customization. Not because the model was stupid. Because the task itself demanded judgment that no one had ever bothered to codify.

Here's what people miss. Those "simple" tasks everyone wanted to automate were never simple. We just paid people poorly to do them and assumed that made the work low-value.

AI exposes who was actually adding value.

Meanwhile, the companies that promised AI would slash headcount are offshoring more than ever. Not less. More. Classic Cobra Effect. They automated the wrong layer, stripped out the humans who were actually pattern-matching in real time, and kept the broken processes intact.

It's not that AI is limited. It's that we keep pointing it at the wrong problems.

Think about what happened with Uber. It didn't replace taxi drivers. It made booking so frictionless that people who never used car services started riding every week. AI is doing the same thing for customer experience right now. Not fewer touchpoints. More of them. Higher quality ones.

The companies that get this will build around cognitively agile people and use AI to strip away busywork. The ones that don't will keep chasing cost savings that never arrive.
