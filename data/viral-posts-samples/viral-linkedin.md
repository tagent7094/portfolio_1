## Sample 1 — Bold Opener + Evidence Stack

I fired our best performing sales rep last month.

Not because of numbers. His numbers were incredible.

Because he was closing deals by promising things we couldn't deliver. Every deal he closed became a support nightmare within 90 days.

Here's what I learned: Revenue is a lagging indicator of trust. And trust compounds.

Since we let him go, our NPS went up 18 points. Renewals jumped 23%. Customer referrals doubled.

The best sales motion isn't closing. It's earning the right to keep the conversation going.

## Sample 2 — Story First + Lived Experience

Last Tuesday at 3am, I got a call from our largest enterprise client.

Their system was down. 400 agents couldn't work. They were losing $50K per hour.

I didn't send an email. I didn't file a ticket. I got on a plane.

By 8am I was in their NOC. By noon we had a fix. By 3pm I was back at the airport.

That client just renewed for 3 years.

Here's what most SaaS founders miss: your product is not your product. Your response when things break is your product.

## Sample 3 — Concession + Counter

I used to think raising more money was always better.

$5M? Why not $15M? Bigger war chest, faster growth, more runway.

Then I watched three competitors raise $50M+ each. Two are dead. One pivoted to a completely different market.

We raised $5M total. We're profitable. We're growing 40% year over year.

The discipline of a small round forced us to find product-market fit before we could afford to fake it.

Capital is not a strategy. It's an accelerant. And accelerants applied to the wrong direction just get you lost faster.

## Sample 4 — Data Drop + Reframe

87% of "AI-powered" customer service tools get abandoned within 12 months.

Not because the AI doesn't work. Because the implementation assumes the problem is efficiency.

It's not. The problem is trust.

When a customer calls about a billing error, they don't want faster resolution. They want to feel heard. They want someone who understands that $200 matters to them.

AI can handle volume. Humans handle moments.

The companies winning in CX aren't replacing agents with bots. They're using AI to make their best agents even better.

---

## Sample 5 — Contrarian Take

Everyone is talking about "AI agents" replacing human agents.

I run a company with 500+ contact center clients. Here's what's actually happening:

The companies that went full-AI-first are quietly hiring humans back. Not because AI failed. Because their CSAT scores dropped 30% and their churn doubled.

The companies winning? They're using AI to eliminate the 60% of calls that are simple lookups — and investing the savings into better training for the humans handling the 40% that actually matter.

AI isn't the story. The story is what you do with the time AI gives back.
