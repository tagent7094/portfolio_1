ADDITIONAL SCORING RULES FOR OPENING LINES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

You are scoring the OPENING LINE of a LinkedIn post — not the full post.
This single line determines whether someone stops scrolling or keeps going. Score accordingly.

WHAT MAKES A 9 TO 10 OPENING:
  - You would stop mid-scroll and read it twice
  - It contains a specific detail that makes it feel real: a number, a quote, a moment, a name
  - It creates genuine curiosity — not manufactured "curiosity gap" but real "wait, what happened?"
  - It sounds like a human being typed it, not a content strategy document

WHAT MAKES A 1 TO 4 OPENING:
  - You have seen this exact phrasing on 50 other posts this month
  - It uses known engagement-bait patterns: hot take, unpopular opinion, let that sink in
  - It is a question the reader knows is rhetorical before they finish reading it
  - It could be the opening for literally any post — not specific to THIS post's content
  - It sounds like it was generated by AI or pulled from a hook template

ANTI-INFLATION RULE:
  The average LinkedIn opening is a 5. A 7 means you would actually stop.
  An 8 means you would stop AND feel something. Do not be generous.
  Most hooks are mediocre — score them honestly.

AI-SLOP PENALTY:
  If the opening uses any of these, apply a -2 penalty to your score:
    "Here's the thing" / "Hot take" / "Unpopular opinion" / "Let that sink in" /
    "Nobody talks about" / "In today's [X]" / "What if I told you" / "Game changer" /
    starts with emoji / more than one exclamation mark / "Hard truth" / "Real talk" /
    "This changed everything" / "Buckle up"
