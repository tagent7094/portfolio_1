PASS 1 — STRIP ALL AI STRUCTURAL TELLS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

AI structures that must be destroyed on sight:

PARALLEL TRIPLETS
  AI loves "X is not about A, it's about B. Y is not about C, it's about D. Z is not about..."
  Humans do this pattern once. Twice at most. Never three times in a row.
  If you see a triplet, collapse two of them or break the pattern with a fragment.

EVEN-HANDED HEDGING
  "While X is important, Y matters too." Founders with real opinions do not write this.
  They assert. Pick a side and state it flat.
  Cut every "on the other hand", "that said", "to be fair", "while X, Y".

NEAT LOGICAL ESCALATION
  AI builds 1-2-3 toward a conclusion like a school essay.
  Humans state the conclusion first, back into the evidence, circle back unexpectedly.
  Scramble the order if it reads too textbook.

UNIFORM PARAGRAPH LENGTH
  If three consecutive paragraphs are within 10 words of each other in length, break it.
  Mix one long paragraph with a one-liner. Then a medium one.
  Paragraph length variation is a human fingerprint.

PERFECT LIST PARALLELISM
  AI lists have identical grammatical structure in every bullet.
  Humans start one item with a noun, another with a verb, another with a number.
  If there is a list, make at least one item structurally different from the others.

THROAT-CLEARING OPENERS
  Cut everything before the real first line.
  "Let me explain...", "Here's what I mean...", "What I mean by this is..."
  These do not exist. The first real sentence is the first sentence.

SUMMARY CLOSINGS
  If the last paragraph restates the first paragraph in different words — delete it.
  The closing should reframe, not summarize. One sharp sentence beats a paragraph of recap.

  utilize -> use

  one must consider -> [delete]

  due to the fact that -> because

FAKE EMOTION NARRATION — humans do not narrate their own feelings this way:
  "I'm excited to share..." -> just share it
  "I'm thrilled to announce..." -> just announce it
  "I'm proud to say..." -> just say it
  "I'm passionate about..." -> show the passion, don't label it

OVERLY PRECISE HEDGING — replace with direct speech:
  "This can sometimes lead to..." -> "This usually means..." or drop the hedge
  "In many cases..." -> "Often..." or just assert
  "It might be worth considering..." -> "Consider..." or delete

SPECIAL CHARACTERS THAT SIGNAL AI-GENERATED TEXT:
  Em dashes (—) -> replace with a period, comma, or line break
  En dashes (–) as connectors -> rewrite the sentence
  Semicolons in casual writing -> split into two sentences
  Excessive colons introducing lists -> use a line break or rewrite
  Parenthetical asides with em dashes -> use actual parentheses or a new sentence
  Bullet points with perfect grammatical parallelism -> break one item's structure
  Bold text on more than 2 phrases per post -> remove all but the most essential
  Ellipsis (...) used for dramatic pause more than once -> cut the extras
  Multiple exclamation marks -> maximum one per post, preferably zero

  If the founder does not use ALL CAPS for emphasis in their real writing, remove it
  Title case in the middle of prose is an AI tell — rewrite to avoid it
