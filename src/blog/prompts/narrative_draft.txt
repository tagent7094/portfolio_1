## FORMAT-SPECIFIC STRUCTURE (all formats are SEO-aware)

### thought_leadership
- H1 title with primary keyword in first 60% of title
- Intro hook (100-150 words) with primary keyword in sentence 1-3
- 5-7 H2 sections, AT LEAST 2 phrased as PAA questions
- 1 bulleted or numbered list inside one section (drives featured snippets)
- 1 comparison frame ("X vs Y") in one section
- FAQ section (H2 "Frequently Asked Questions" with 4-6 H3 questions) before conclusion
- Conclusion H2 with founder-voice CTA ("Ask yourself...")

### behind_the_scenes
- H1 with primary keyword
- Scene-setting opener (150-200 words) anchored to a specific verified moment from transcript
- 4-6 H2 sections walking through the story arc
- One H2 must be a "what I learned" or "the lessons" structured list
- FAQ section with 3-5 H3 questions
- Conclusion H2 tying back to the opening scene

### listicle
- H1 with "[Number] [Primary Keyword] [Outcome]" pattern (e.g., "7 Reasons JEE Is a Scam")
- Intro (100-150 words) with primary keyword and brief credibility anchor
- 5-8 numbered H2 items (e.g., "## 1. [Item heading]")
- Each item 150-300 words with concrete example or founder anchor
- Conclusion H2 summarizing the list's through-line
- Optional FAQ section before conclusion

### how_to
- H1 with "How to [Primary Keyword]" pattern
- Intro (100-150 words) with primary keyword and stakes
- "What you need" H2 (bulleted list of prerequisites)
- 5-7 H2 step sections (e.g., "## Step 1: [Action]")
- "Common mistakes" H2 (bulleted list)
- FAQ section with 4-5 H3 questions
- Conclusion H2

### comparison
- H1 with "[X] vs [Y]: [Primary Keyword]" pattern
- Intro (100-150 words) framing the choice
- "At a glance" H2 with comparison TABLE (drives featured snippets hard)
- 4-6 H2 sections, each examining one dimension of comparison
- "Which should you choose?" H2 with decision criteria
- FAQ section with 3-5 questions
- Conclusion H2

## MANDATORY OUTPUT RULES

### Voice (non-negotiable)
1. Use actual quotes from the transcript as anchors. Rewrite surrounding context in the founder's blog voice.
2. The recognition test: would the founder read this and think "this captures what I meant"?
3. No corporate language. No AI slop. No "in today's landscape." No "let that sink in."
4. Blog voice = slightly more expansive than LinkedIn, but same register and conviction.
5. Hit the word target (±15%).
6. Use at least 4 of the founder's documented voice markers naturally across the post.

### SEO (non-negotiable)
1. MINIMUM 5 H2 headings in body. Anything less and the blog won't rank.
2. AT LEAST 2 H2 headings drawn from people_also_ask_targets.
3. Primary keyword appears 4-8 times in body (NOT keyword-stuffed).
4. EVERY related entity appears at least once.
5. AT LEAST 3 of the long_tail_variations appear in the body.
6. AT LEAST 3 structured elements present (FAQ, list, comparison table, definition box, numbered steps).
7. FAQ section is mandatory if search_intent = informational.
8. Title is 50-65 characters with primary keyword in first half.
9. Meta description is 140-155 characters with primary keyword once.
10. First paragraph contains primary keyword in sentence 1-3.
