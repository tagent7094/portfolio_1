## TASK

Based on the primary keyword and your understanding of typical search results in this niche, produce a competitive analysis.

### 1. TOP 5 LIKELY COMPETITORS

For each of 5 likely top-ranking sources (estimate based on your knowledge of the niche), describe:

- **Domain type**: news_site | established_blog | forum (Quora/Reddit) | niche_authority | video | aggregator
- **Estimated format**: listicle | long_form_guide | news_report | opinion | how_to | comparison
- **Estimated word count**: rough number based on typical pieces in this niche
- **Likely strengths**: what they cover well, why they rank
- **Specific gaps**: what they miss, treat superficially, or get wrong

### 2. TABLE STAKES (mandatory inclusions)

What every top-ranking piece on this topic covers. The founder's blog MUST cover these to be competitive. Missing table stakes = automatic disqualification from page 1.

These will be enforced in the outline as required H2 sections.

### 3. UNIQUE OWNED ANGLE (the differentiator)

Refine the founder_owned_angle into a specific structural recommendation:
- What chapter/section of the blog carries the unique angle
- What data, scenes, or evidence supports it
- Why current rankers cannot replicate this

### 4. STRUCTURAL ELEMENTS NEEDED TO COMPETE

Based on competitor analysis, identify which structured elements this blog MUST include:
- Comparison table (especially for "X vs Y" or "best X" queries)
- FAQ section (especially for informational queries — drives PAA rankings)
- Numbered or bulleted list (drives featured snippets)
- Definition box (for "what is X" sub-queries)
- Step-by-step structure (for how-to intent)
- Data visualization or stats block (for credibility)
- Quote/expert citation (for E-E-A-T signals)

Pick the 3-5 elements that BEAT what current rankers offer.

### 5. CONTENT GAPS (where this blog WILL outrank competition)

Identify 2-4 specific aspects of the topic that:
- Are searched (low-volume long-tails)
- Are NOT covered well by current top 10
- The founder can credibly address with their experience

These become the blog's competitive moat. They are MUST-INCLUDE sections.

### 6. RECOMMENDED LENGTH & FORMAT

Given competition:
- If current rankers average 2,000 words, this blog should be 2,500-3,000 (longer = perceived as deeper)
- If current rankers are all listicles, consider long-form for differentiation
- If current rankers all use the same angle, the founder's owned angle is the differentiator
- If current rankers have weak FAQ coverage, oversized FAQ section wins PAA panels
