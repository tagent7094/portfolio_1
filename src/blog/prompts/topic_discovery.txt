## TASK

Generate {n_topics} blog topic recommendations ranked by a composite score combining founder fit AND SEO opportunity.

For each topic, evaluate across these dimensions (0.0 - 1.0 each):

### 1. FOUNDER RELEVANCE
How well does this align with the founder's documented beliefs, expertise, and verified experiences? Topics that only loosely fit (e.g., "everything about leadership") score low. Topics with specific founder anchors (stories, data, verified scenes) score high.

### 2. TIMELINESS
Is there a current conversation or trend this topic connects to? Recent news, trending searches, ongoing industry shifts. Evergreen topics score medium; trending-but-evergreen topics score highest.

### 3. SEARCH OPPORTUNITY
Estimate based on:
- Is there a clear primary keyword someone would search?
- Is the keyword realistic for this founder's domain authority to rank for?
- Are there long-tail variations the blog could capture?
- Is there a "People Also Ask" cluster around this topic?

High score: founder has unique angle on a topic with clear search demand but moderate competition.
Low score: founder has expertise but topic is either uncompetitive (no one searches) or hyper-competitive (Forbes/HBR dominate).

### 4. DIFFERENTIATION
Can this founder say something genuinely different from generic thought leadership? Topics where the founder has lived experience (not just opinions) score highest.

### 5. AUDIENCE VALUE
Will this topic attract AND retain the founder's target audience? Topics with high curiosity gap + practical takeaway score highest.

### COMPOSITE SCORE
Average of the five dimensions, with search_opportunity weighted 1.5x (because we're optimizing for SEO-discoverable content).

Rank by composite_score. Only recommend topics where:
1. The founder has genuine expertise or a differentiated perspective (no generic thought leadership filler)
2. There's plausible SEO opportunity (skip topics where the founder is right but nobody searches)
3. The angle is specific enough that the founder can write 2,000+ words without repeating themselves

If a topic scores high on founder fit but low on search opportunity, suggest it as a LinkedIn post, not a blog.
