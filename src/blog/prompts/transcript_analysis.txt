## TASK

Extract everything from this transcript that could become a compelling blog post. Focus EXCLUSIVELY on what is discussed in the transcript — do not infer topics not present.

### 1. KEY THEMES (3-8 themes)

What are the major themes discussed? For each:
- Theme title
- 2-3 sentence summary of what was actually said
- Strength rating (strong/medium/weak) based on:
  - How much time/depth the speaker gave it
  - Whether they made a clear, defensible claim
  - Whether they backed it with specific evidence
- Estimated SEO potential (high/medium/low) based on:
  - Whether the theme maps to a searchable query
  - Whether competition is moderate (not Wikipedia-level dominated)
  - Whether the founder has a unique angle vs. generic takes

### 2. MEMORABLE QUOTES (5-15 quotes)

Direct quotes from the transcript that are punchy, insightful, or contrarian. For each:
- Exact text (verbatim — do not paraphrase)
- Speaker
- Context: what prompted this statement
- Usability rating (high/medium/low) for blog use
- Could anchor a blog: yes/no — would this quote work as the opening or closing line of a blog post?

### 3. STORIES & ANECDOTES (any present)

Personal experiences, case studies, or specific examples shared. For each:
- Story label
- What happened (2-4 sentences)
- Emotional register (controlled_anger / quiet_authority / vulnerability / generosity / measured_critique)
- Blog potential (high/medium/low)
- Verified scene? Yes/no — is this a story the founder has documented before, or a new one?

### 4. CONTRARIAN POSITIONS

Opinions that explicitly go against conventional wisdom. For each:
- The contrarian take (one sentence)
- The conventional wisdom it pushes against
- Evidence from transcript backing the position
- Strength: how defensibly stated (strong/medium/weak)
- SEO opportunity: do people search for the contrarian angle? (e.g., "why is X bad" gets searched)

### 5. ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS

Practical advice or frameworks mentioned. For each:
- The practical takeaway (one sentence)
- Target audience (who benefits most)
- Specificity level (specific framework / general principle)

### 6. SEARCHABLE QUESTIONS RAISED

Questions the speaker explicitly asks OR implicitly addresses that real people would search for. These map to "People Also Ask" targets later.

Examples:
- "Why is JEE coaching so expensive?"
- "Is the Indian education system broken?"
- "What skills do founders actually look for?"

### 7. NAMED ENTITIES (for topical authority)

Specific names, companies, products, places, frameworks mentioned. These become "related entities" the blog must reference for topical depth.
