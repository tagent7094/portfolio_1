VIRAL POST ADAPTATION FRAMEWORK — READ AND EXECUTE IN ORDER
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
This framework governs the entire generation. Execute each step in sequence. Do not skip steps. If any instruction elsewhere conflicts with this framework, this framework wins.

── STEP 1: INTERNALIZE THE FOUNDER ──

Read every piece of {founder_context}, {beliefs}, and {personality_card} before writing a single line.

- Map the recurring TENSIONS the founder returns to — not topics, but collisions. Collisions are opposing forces the founder keeps navigating.
- Identify their SIGNATURE SCENES — the physical moments, specific conversations, and anchoring details they reuse across posts.
- Note their ARGUMENT RHYTHM: do they spiral inward (broad → personal → concrete moment), build linearly, or toggle between personal and universal?
- Find moments where the founder is EXPOSED, NOT IN CONTROL — those are the best raw material for post bodies.
- Map their RECURRING CAST — names, relationships, origin stories they reference.
- Study the founder's TYPICAL WORD COUNT RANGE across their posts. Every generated post must land within this range. Length is voice. Overshooting word count turns a punchy voice into an essay and breaks authenticity.

── STEP 2: DISSECT THE SOURCE POST ──

Identify the NARRATIVE ARC of {original_text} — what movement does the source post run on?

Break the opening hook into its MECHANICAL PARTS. Identify each component — what is the first sentence doing? What is the second sentence doing? Is there a permission frame, a diagnostic, a consequence, a reframe? Label each part by its STRUCTURAL FUNCTION, not its content.

Map the EXACT SENTENCE COUNT, RHYTHM, AND STRUCTURAL FUNCTION of the opening lines.

Then identify 5 SITUATIONS FROM THE FOUNDER'S WORLD where that arc fits naturally.

── STEP 3: MIRROR THE OPENING HOOK ──

The opening lines are load-bearing — they carry the virality. Mirror the source post's MECHANIC, not its words.

- Same sentence count.
- Same rhythm.
- Same structural function per sentence.

Substitute the founder's world into the slot where the original creator's world sits. The role changes, the time changes, the situation changes, the domain changes. THE MOLD STAYS IDENTICAL.

Get creative with WHAT fills the structure, not the structure itself. If it reads like anyone could have written it, it's too close to the source. If it doesn't follow the mechanic, it loses the virality engine.

Same bones, different skin.

MANDATE HIERARCHY: If a style rule from {style_rules} (e.g., "paradoxical opening") conflicts with the structural mold from this step (sentence count, rhythm, tension type), the structural mold wins. Style rules govern word choice and register WITHIN the mold. They never override the mold itself.

── STEP 4: CLOSE THE SOURCE POST AFTER THE HOOK ──

Once the opening lines are written, DO NOT LOOK AT {original_text} AGAIN.

Build everything after the hook entirely from the founder's world — their stories, their reasoning, their way of moving through an argument.

If the body starts feeling like a remix of the source post, you're still reading it. Stop. Return to the founder's scenes.

── STEP 4b: THE HOOK IS A PROMISE. THE BODY MUST DELIVER IT. ──

The opening hook sets a specific argument in motion. The body must follow THAT arc — not pivot to adjacent founder material that happens to fit the topic.

Before writing the body, restate the hook's promise in one sentence. Then check: does every paragraph advance THIS specific argument? If the body drifts to a related-but-different point, it breaks the post's internal logic.

── STEP 5: BODY MUST ORBIT A VISIBLE MOMENT ──

Every post needs at least one SCENE YOU CAN PICTURE — a physical moment, a specific conversation, a concrete detail.

- The founder must be INSIDE the experience, not observing others.
- VULNERABILITY BEATS AUTHORITY — moments where the founder is exposed, not in control, outperform moments where they lecture.
- Posts that only analyze patterns without personal stakes get cut.
- Abstract pattern recognition without an anchoring scene doesn't land.

CRITICAL: Do not reassemble paragraphs from the founder's existing posts. Use their scenes and collisions as raw ingredients, not pre-cooked meals. If a sentence could be copy-pasted from one of their published posts, rewrite it until it can't.

── STEP 6: ENDINGS LAND ON A SPECIFIC DETAIL ──

Never restate the thesis. Never summarize. Never CTA.

End on: a concrete image, a name, a challenge thrown at the reader, something unresolved, a quiet admission, or a physical object.

TEST: Does the last line leave a picture or a lesson? Pictures win.
