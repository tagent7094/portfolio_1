You are extracting the VOCABULARY FINGERPRINT of a specific person — the words and phrases they habitually use AND the words they conspicuously avoid.

This is critical for ghostwriting: signature phrases make content feel authentic, and banned words prevent content from sounding AI-generated.

## TEXT TO ANALYZE

{text}

## WHAT TO EXTRACT

### 1. Signature Phrases (phrases_used)
Words, phrases, and transitions this person uses REPEATEDLY. These are recognition markers — a colleague would recognize these as "theirs."

Known examples to look for:
- "Here's what people miss..."
- "Here's what separates them..."
- "Here's the second-order effect..."
- "Here's what's actually happening..."
- "Stop [X]. Start [Y]."
- "It's not [X]. It's [Y]."
- "Meanwhile..."
- "I'll take [X] over [Y] every single time"
- "You know what that required?"
- "Always remember:"

Also extract: recurring metaphors, go-to analogies, brand-specific terms they use naturally.

### 2. Banned Words (phrases_never)
Words and patterns this person NEVER uses or that would make their writing sound AI-generated:

Known bans:
- Em-dashes (—) — they use hyphens (-) or en-dashes (–)
- "leverage" as a verb
- "navigate", "landscape", "foster", "harness", "unlock", "elevate", "empower"
- "game-changer", "paradigm shift", "deep dive", "at scale"
- "resonate", "align", "synergy", "holistic", "robust"
- "In today's [anything]", "In the world of [anything]"
- "Let that sink in", "Read that again"
- Hashtags
- Emojis (except very rare strategic use)
- Hedge words: "maybe", "perhaps", "I think", "arguably"
- Engagement bait: "What do you think?", "Thoughts?", "Like if you agree"
- Research citations: "Studies show", "According to McKinsey"

### 3. Pronoun Rules (pronoun_rules)
How they use I / we / you / they:
- "I" for personal experience and lived lessons — authority through firsthand knowledge
- "we" for team wins and company milestones — sharing credit, never "I built this alone"
- "you" for direct reader address — creates peer conversation, never condescending
- "they" for broken systems and misaligned incentives — distancing from mainstream

### 4. Punctuation Rules (punctuation_rules)
- Commas to join related thoughts in the same breath — momentum, not separation
- Periods for verdicts — short declarative sentences that close arguments
- Parenthetical asides for grounded specificity: "(be it product, sales, or design)"
- Colons to introduce lists or explanations — clean transitions
- Hyphens (-) ONLY. No em-dashes (—). Ever.
- No semicolons in argument posts — too formal
- Exclamation marks only in celebratory/milestone posts, never philosophy posts
- Occasional :) in lighter posts — authentic, not performative
- Very rare CAPS for emphasis (e.g., "REALITY") — only when frustration is the point, never for style
