You are analyzing the WRITING STYLE of a specific person. Extract the structural patterns, rhetorical moves, and voice constraints that define HOW they write.

## THE 12 ESSENTIAL PATTERNS TO LOOK FOR

### TIER 1: STRUCTURAL (How Posts Are Built)
1. **Paradoxical Opening** — Starts with something that FEELS WRONG. [Desired trait] = [Disaster outcome].
   Example: "The most dangerous hires I've made were the ones who had all the answers"
2. **Accumulative Evidence** — NOT linear (A→B→C) but STACKING (A + A + A). Each sentence reinforces the SAME point until it's undeniable.
3. **Signature Phrases** — Exact transitions they reuse: "Here's what people miss...", "Here's what separates them...", "Here's the second-order effect..."
4. **"Meanwhile" Ironic Contrast** — [What should happen] + "Meanwhile" + [Ironic opposite].

### TIER 2: RHYTHM (How Sentences Flow)
5. **Rhetorical Question → Immediate Answer** — Question creates tension, answer is SHORT (3-8 words max).
6. **"Not X, Y" Correction** — "It's not [what people think]. It's [what it actually is]."
7. **Setup-Punch Combo** — Longer buildup → 5-10 word declarative punch.
8. **Extreme Compression** — Contractions ALWAYS, 15-18 word max sentences, delete every word that doesn't carry weight.

### TIER 3: TONE (How It Sounds)
9. **Present Tense Observation** — "I see this pattern" not "I saw this pattern"
10. **Earned Cynicism** — Cynical about narratives and trends, constructive about actions and solutions. NOT bitter.
11. **Casual Authority Blend** — "Here's what I've learned...", "I'll take X over Y every time"
12. **Negative Definitions** — "Stop X. Start Y." Define success by what NOT to do.

## VOICE CONSTRAINTS (EQUALLY IMPORTANT)
- Hyphens (-) not em-dashes (—)
- Sentence rhythm FLOWS with commas, PIVOTS with "but" — NOT staccato punch-punch-punch
- No hashtags
- No emojis (except very rare strategic use)
- No hedge words ("maybe", "perhaps", "I think", "arguably")
- No engagement bait ("What do you think?", "Thoughts?")
- No bullet point lists in prose
- Contractions everywhere ("I've" not "I have")

## SENTENCE RHYTHM PATTERNS (from Voice DNA)
- **Certainty compresses** — When certain, sentences get SHORT and declarative. When building a story, sentences breathe with detail and specificity.
- **Commas for momentum** — Two thoughts in the same exhale get a comma. A verdict gets a period. This creates a reading rhythm that feels like thinking out loud.
- **Three-beat stack** — Builds pressure through parallel structure, almost always three beats. By the third, conviction is established through accumulation, not logic.
- **Concession-then-counter** — Gives ground ("Sure, X. But Y.") before taking it back harder. The concession makes the counter feel fair and earned.

## OPENING LINE MECHANICS
Extract these as rule_type="opening":
- **Bold declarative** — Immediate tension in a single sentence. No build-up. Sounds like mid-argument at a dinner table.
- **Personal number + contrarian claim** — "I've built companies worth $2B+ combined..." followed by a stance no one expects.
- **The reframe opener** — Names a familiar thing, then reveals it's actually something else entirely.
- **NEVER opens with:** a question, someone else's quote, "Everyone says...", soft build to a punch, "In today's..."

## CLOSING LINE MECHANICS
Extract these as rule_type="closing":
- **The verdict** — Single line that reframes or elevates everything above. Not a summary — a conclusion the reader didn't expect.
- **Operational directive with philosophical weight** — A concrete instruction that carries deeper meaning.
- **NEVER closes with:** "What do you think?", a CTA, inspirational one-liner, "Like if you agree", softened landing, or hashtags.

## ANTI-PATTERNS TO FLAG (from Ghostwriting System)
When extracting style_rules, also capture these as anti_pattern entries:
### Structural anti-patterns:
- Mirrored parallel structures in pairs/triples (one is fine, two+ is an AI flag)
- Staccato fragment chains ("Not retention. Not performance. Seats." — sounds like a machine)
- Perfect symmetry in contrasts (real speech is asymmetric — one side gets more detail)
- Clean one-liner closer for every section (formulaic)
### Language anti-patterns:
- Rhetorical questions as transitions ("But what does that really mean?")
- Product-page phrasing that could appear on a SaaS landing page
- Hedge stacking (two+ qualifiers in sequence: "arguably perhaps somewhat")
### Tone anti-patterns:
- Performing authority instead of demonstrating it (asserting expertise vs. showing it)
- Apologetic framing ("I know this is controversial but...")
- Celebrating own insight ("And that's when I realized something profound")

## WHAT IS NOT A STYLE RULE (do NOT extract these)
❌ "Believes AI augments rather than replaces" — that's a BELIEF
❌ "Analyzes incentive structures" — that's a THINKING MODEL
❌ "Tells story about fundraising" — that's a STORY
