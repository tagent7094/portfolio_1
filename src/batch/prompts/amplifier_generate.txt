## THE 13 PROVEN MECHANICS
Each version must use a different one. Pick the 5 best fits for this post:
1. Specific number + context — lead with a precise figure from the draft. "$412K closed last quarter. Target was $350K."
2. Juxtaposition — two things that shouldn't coexist in the same sentence. "Our competitor raised $40M. We outshipped them with 3 engineers."
3. First-person confession — admit something with real cost. "I told 50 buyers we were ready. I was wrong."
4. Quote hook + reaction — someone said something, then your reaction. "'We need you to present to the supervisor.' That's when I knew the deal was dead."
5. Scene-entry — start in the middle of a physical moment. "We walked into their call center. Post-it notes on every monitor."
6. Decision reveal — the outcome of a hard choice, stated plainly. "A Fortune 500 asked us to build a CRM. We said no."
7. Contrarian declaration — state something most would disagree with (earned, not clickbait). "Most founder advice is wrong."
8. Pattern observation — notice something others miss. "Every founder I know who's crossed $20M runs into the same wall."
9. Tension pair — name two forces pulling in opposite directions. "The market says automate everything. Customers keep asking for humans."
10. Before/after snapshot — two states separated by time, the contrast implies the story. "Two years ago we couldn't close a single deal. Last month we turned down three."
11. Micro story — a complete narrative arc in 1-2 sentences. "An investor asked what happens if a competitor copies us. I showed him our NPS scores."
12. Authority redirect — cite a credible external authority, then redirect their point. "McKinsey says 70% of transformations fail. They left out why the 30% succeed."
13. Data inversion — take a commonly cited stat and show it means the opposite. "95% customer satisfaction. We were measuring the wrong customers."

## WHAT TO REMOVE OR CONVERT
- "Excited to announce…" → the specific number or outcome
- "I've been thinking about…" → the conclusion. Delete the preamble.
- "What's your X strategy?" → "Most teams answer this wrong."
- "X is important" → "[Specific outcome] when X is missing."
- "Here's what nobody tells you…" → the thing. Direct.
- Multiple exclamation marks → one or zero
- Stat-stack fragments → a sentence containing the stat
- Strawman quotes → a pattern observation

## HIGH-LIFT ELEMENTS (add 1-2 per opener, NOT all)
Adding 3+ creates stat-stacking, which reads as ad copy. The test: if the opener reads as a sentence a person would speak in conversation, fine. If it reads as bullet points compressed into one line, over-loaded.
1. A specific number FROM the draft (don't invent)
2. A first-person verb — "I lost," "we shipped," "I told." Beats "companies should."
3. A juxtaposition — two facts in the draft that create dissonance when placed together
4. An emotion word — "tired," "wrong," "painful," "brutal" beats "interesting." Earned, not manufactured.
5. A named entity FROM the draft only — don't introduce new names.
