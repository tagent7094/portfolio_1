## THE 8 PROVEN MECHANICS
Each version must use a different one:
1. Specific number + context — lead with a precise figure from the draft
2. Juxtaposition — two things that shouldn't coexist in the same sentence
3. First-person confession — admit something with real cost
4. Quote hook + reaction — someone said something, then your reaction
5. Scene-entry — start in the middle of a physical moment
6. Decision reveal — the outcome of a hard choice, stated plainly
7. Contrarian declaration — state something most would disagree with (earned, not clickbait)
8. Pattern observation — notice something others miss

## WHAT TO REMOVE OR CONVERT
- "Excited to announce…" → the specific number or outcome
- "I've been thinking about…" → the conclusion. Delete the preamble.
- "What's your X strategy?" → "Most teams answer this wrong."
- "X is important" → "[Specific outcome] when X is missing."
- "Here's what nobody tells you…" → the thing. Direct.
- Multiple exclamation marks → one or zero
- Stat-stack fragments → a sentence containing the stat
- Strawman quotes → a pattern observation

## HIGH-LIFT ELEMENTS (add 1-2 per opener, NOT all)
Adding 3+ creates stat-stacking, which reads as ad copy. The test: if the opener reads as a sentence a person would speak in conversation, fine. If it reads as bullet points compressed into one line, over-loaded.
1. A specific number FROM the draft (don't invent)
2. A first-person verb — "I lost," "we shipped," "I told." Beats "companies should."
3. A juxtaposition — two facts in the draft that create dissonance when placed together
4. An emotion word — "tired," "wrong," "painful," "brutal" beats "interesting." Earned, not manufactured.
5. A named entity FROM the draft only — don't introduce new names.

## RULES FOR EACH VERSION
- Must pass all 5 gates (mechanic deployed well, plausible, coherent with body, specific, mode-preserving)
- Gate 2 (plausibility): No strawman quotes, no counted-precision tics, no convenient setups, no impossible scenarios
- Gate 3 (coherence): The opener's promise must match what the body delivers — not adjacent, not sharper
- Gate 4 (specificity): Default to third-degree patterns when specifics aren't in the draft. Never invent first-degree.
- Gate 5 (mode): If {mode} is "wrestling," opener must signal movement. If "declaring," signal certainty.
- Must sound like this specific founder (use voice markers)
- No AI slop patterns (no "In today's...", "Here's the thing", "Hot take:", etc.)
