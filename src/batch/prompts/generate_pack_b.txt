## ASSIGNED ENTRY DOOR: {entry_door}
You MUST use this specific entry door for the opening. Entry door definitions:
- scene_drop: Start in the MIDDLE of a specific physical moment. No setup, no context.
- diagnostic_question: Open with a question that makes the reader self-diagnose.
- borrowed_authority_quote: Open with something someone credible said, then react. The quote must pass plausibility — would this person actually say this?
- data_contradiction: Two data points that shouldn't both be true.
- confession: Admit something that costs you credibility. Not fake vulnerability where the "confession" makes you look good.
- physical_object: Anchor on a specific physical thing (a laptop, a whiteboard, a text message on a screen).
- direct_address: Talk directly to a specific type of person ("If you're a first-time founder...").
- mocked_counter_example: State the bad version of what people do, then flip.
- contrarian_claim: State something most people would disagree with. Earned, not clickbait.
- parallel_structure: Two or three parallel sentences with a twist in the last one.
- age_time_anchor: Open with a specific age, date, or time marker that signals real memory.
- second_party_verdict: Someone else's judgment or reaction to your situation.

If two doors feel functionally similar in execution, treat them as the same door — the test is whether a reader experiencing both hooks back-to-back would feel a meaningful shift in approach.

## BATCH B RULES

1. PSYCHOLOGICAL MOVE must match the source's primary mechanic ({hook_mechanic_primary}) but expressed through a completely different surface.

2. ENTRY DOOR: Use the assigned door above. The opening sentences must clearly employ this entry type.

3. ANTI-RECYCLING:
   - If a sentence could be copy-pasted from one of the founder's published posts, rewrite it until it can't.
   - A punchline they've used must be earned through different setup, or dropped entirely.
   - The founder's posts are evidence of who they are — not a parts bin.

4. EVERY POST MUST:
   - Hit the word count range (±30% of median)
   - Match the founder's formatting habits EXACTLY (formatting mismatch is the biggest AI tell)
   - Orbit one scene the reader can physically picture
   - Put the founder INSIDE the experience — vulnerable, exposed, not watching from outside
   - Deliver exactly what the hook promised — every paragraph advances THAT argument
   - End on a concrete detail — a name, an image, a physical object, a challenge, a quiet admission. NOT a summary, NOT a moral, NOT a thesis restatement
   - Not read like a rearrangement of an existing founder post

5. ENDING CHECK:
   If the ending feels over-explained, cut the second-to-last paragraph.

6. PLAUSIBILITY: Any quote, scene, or dialogue must pass the dinner test — would this person actually say this in real life? No strawman quotes, no convenient setup lines, no counted-precision tics.

7. SPECIFICITY:
   - First-degree (real names, real numbers) — only from the founder's published claims
   - Second-degree (anonymized but scaled correctly) — only if founder has companies/experience at that scale
   - Third-degree (pattern observations) — always safe, default when in doubt

8. MODE: Specify "wrestling" or "declaring". Vary across B variants — not all the same mode.
   - Wrestling: exploring tension, uncertain, the turn is the point
   - Declaring: stating position, confident, defending in a straight line

9. ACROSS ALL 9 POSTS: No two deliver the same argument. The collision can be familiar. The argument built from it must be new.
