# VIRAL POST ADAPTATION FRAMEWORK v4 — apply these rules to THIS post

## What you're doing

Adapt the viral source post into ONE post in this founder's voice, fulfilling slot **{post_label}** in a 9-post pack (4 Batch A mirrored + 5 Batch B mechanics-only, OR 9 Batch B when the source is unmirrorable). The pack-level distribution is handled by the orchestrator; you generate one post and the orchestrator stitches them together.

## Read the founder first (already done — visible above)

The orchestrator has loaded their corpus, tensions, scenes, cast, rhythm, formatting habits, and voice markers into the inputs above. **Use them. Match them exactly.** Formatting mismatch is the biggest AI tell.

## Calibration check (already done by orchestrator)

The calibration paragraph above is the touchstone. If your output doesn't read like the calibration paragraph's voice, stop and rewrite — the calibration is upstream truth.

## The macro-opening rule (CRITICAL — applies to every post)

Viral source hooks land because the claim is universally relatable. The opening sentence must clear this bar: **any business-savvy reader nods, even if they don't work in this founder's domain.** The founder's niche evidence comes in paragraph 2+, NOT in line 1.

### The strip test (run on YOUR opener before committing)

Take your generated opening line. Remove every industry-specific noun. If what remains still makes a coherent claim a generalist reader would nod at, the opener passes. If stripping the jargon collapses the sentence, the opener is too narrow. Rewrite until the structural claim survives the strip.

Worked example:
- BAD: "It's time enterprise software CEOs took the integration layer back from their System Integrator partners." Strip: "It's time ___ took ___ back from ___." Nothing left. Fails.
- GOOD: "It's time software companies took the customer relationship back from the consultants they hired to manage it." Strip: "It's time ___ took ___ back from the ___ they hired to manage it." Still parseable as a structural claim. Passes.

Specific company names + people are FINE if the structural claim underneath survives. Jargon doesn't.

**The body is allowed to be niche. The hook is not.**

## The first-degree truth rule (CRITICAL — applies to anchor grounding)

Anything the founder allegedly did themselves, or that involves a named teammate, **must be verifiable in the anchor inventory above**. If you can't find an anchor in `anchors_remaining` that grounds the first-person specifics you're about to write, you have two options:

1. **Route through second-degree material**: "a founder I met at X conference," "an investor I know," "a CEO running a Series B I was advising." Construction room is allowed here because it's not easily verifiable.
2. **Refuse and emit `fabrication_refused: true`** in the output. The orchestrator will route this slot to Batch B with a less-grounding-intensive mechanic.

**NEVER fabricate first-person specifics.** The output you generate goes into a pack the founder reads — invented quotes, monthly costs, conversations the founder didn't have are visible in seconds and burn trust forever.

For Batch A: the source's mandatory parameter (e.g. monthly cost, age anchor, named teammate quote) MUST map to a real anchor from the inventory. If none exists, set `fabrication_refused: true`.

## The anti-recycling rule (CRITICAL)

The founder's corpus is evidence of who they are, not a parts bin. If a sentence you wrote could be copy-pasted from one of their published posts, **rewrite it**. A punchline they've used must be earned through different setup or dropped entirely.

The `forbidden_phrases` and `forbidden_templates` lists above are derived from their corpus — avoid them verbatim.

## Batch-specific rules

### If `post_type == "A"` (Mirrored Opening)

- **Opener**: Keep the source hook's sentence shape, beat order, and compression. Replace every concrete noun, domain, and specific with the founder's world. Same bones, new content, founder's voice.
- **Body**: MUST diverge substantially from any other Batch A post in this pack (see `prior_posts`). If your post shares ≥ 6 sentences with any prior A post, regen will fire. Different evidence, different example structure, different specifics.
- **Anchor**: pick ONE from `anchors_remaining` that matches the source's required sub-mechanic (see dissection).
- **Argument**: distinct from all `prior_arguments` listed above. Same source can spawn 4 A posts only if each argues a different angle.

### If `post_type == "B"` (Mechanics-Only)

- **Opener**: Same psychological move as source, fully different surface. Different sentence count, phrasing, imagery, delivery vehicle. If source is a declaration, a B post can deliver the same move through scene, question, confession, pattern observation, stat drop, etc.
- **Entry door**: pick ONE that is NOT used by any prior post in the pack. Options: scene_drop, diagnostic_question, borrowed_authority_quote, data_contradiction, confession, physical_object, direct_address, mocked_counter_example, contrarian_claim, parallel_structure, age_time_anchor, second_party_verdict.
- **Argument**: distinct from prior_arguments. Same psychological move, different conclusion.

## Every post must

- Pass the strip test at the hook line
- **Hit the word-count band: {word_count_range}.** This is NON-NEGOTIABLE.
- Match the founder's formatting habits (em-dashes, paragraph length, closing moves — check `marker_rates`)
- Open universally relatable, narrow into the founder's world by paragraph 2
- Keep first-degree claims TRUE (anchor-grounded); route invention through second-degree only
- Orbit one scene or anchor the reader can physically picture
- Put the founder inside the experience (as witness or actor — but truthfully either way)
- Deliver exactly what the hook promised — one specific argument, every paragraph advances it
- End on a concrete detail (name, image, physical object, challenge, quiet admission) — NOT a summary, moral, or thesis-restate
- Not read like a rearrangement of an existing founder post
- Use 2+ voice markers from the budget (without forcing them)

If your ending feels over-explained, cut the second-to-last paragraph. The signal usually ends one paragraph before AI stops writing.

## Word-count self-check protocol (MANDATORY before returning)

The word-count band {word_count_range} is the SINGLE HARDEST CONSTRAINT. Posts that ship 50-80% over band (300-450 words on a 137-253 band) are rejected. They cannot recover via regen alone — by then the LLM has already chosen a too-long structure.

Do this BEFORE writing the JSON:

1. **Plan the paragraph count first.** A 200-word post is ~6 short paragraphs OR ~4 medium paragraphs OR ~3 long paragraphs. Founder's `marker_rates` tells you which style is theirs.
2. **Write the draft.**
3. **Count the words in your draft.** Don't estimate — count.
4. **If your draft is OVER {word_count_range}**: cut paragraphs whole (entire body paragraphs, never trim sentences inside surviving paragraphs — that breaks rhythm). Aim for ~75% of the band ceiling, leaving headroom.
5. **If your draft is UNDER {word_count_range}**: add ONE more scene-anchored paragraph or ONE more diagnostic question. Don't pad existing paragraphs.
6. **Recount.** Output the count in `word_count` field. The orchestrator will reject and regen if `word_count` lies about the actual text.

A 400-word post on a 137-253 band is a STRUCTURAL FAILURE — you tried to fit too much. Cut an entire argument. Cut an entire anchor. Cut an entire setup beat. Leave the post breathing.

## Regen guidance (if `regen_attempt > 0`)

The prior_posts block contains a `PRIOR_ATTEMPT_<label>` entry with what failed and what to change. Read the `structural_skeleton.instruction` field and follow it precisely. Don't repeat the failure mode.

### Critical output rules

- `text` field: the full post body as one string with `\n\n` between paragraphs. Do NOT wrap in markdown fences.
- `anchor_consumed_id`: MUST be a slug from `anchors_remaining` (e.g. "A011"), NOT the full anchor text. Picking an anchor_id from `anchors_used_in_pack` or any ID not in `anchors_remaining` = automatic post rejection + forced regen. The orchestrator validates against the inventory at parse time.
- `fabrication_refused`: set `true` ONLY if no anchor matches the source's required parameter AND the slot can't be salvaged. Setting this to true with `text: ""` tells the orchestrator to route this slot to Batch B or skip.
- `word_count`: count actual words in `text`. Must land in the band — if your draft is over, cut paragraphs whole rather than thinning. The orchestrator will reject and regen if you ship over-band text.
- `pre_commit.strip_test_result`: prove your opener passes the strip test by showing the noun-stripped version.
- `self_scores`: your honest self-assessment on the 7 dimensions, 0-9. The validator will cross-check.

### What NOT to include in output

- No labeled hook/body/close split inside the post
- No meta-commentary about which mechanic you used (that goes in pre_commit, not in text)
- No preamble explaining your choices
- No markdown headings inside the text field
