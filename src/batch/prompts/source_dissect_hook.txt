## TASK: Dissect the Hook

### 1. THE PSYCHOLOGICAL MOVE
Figure out what the hook does to the reader — the psychological move. Examples:
- Three-stack pain escalation (old pain + new pain + meta-trap for objecting)
- Second-party verdict that reframes the speaker's whole career
- Contrarian claim that inverts conventional wisdom
- Specific number or age anchor that signals real memory
- Authority figure delivering a quote the reader doesn't expect
- Parallel data points with a third-line reveal
- Scene drop that puts the reader inside a moment mid-action
- Diagnostic question that makes the reader self-categorize
- Confession that costs the speaker credibility

The move is what carries over to the generated posts. Everything else is surface.

### 2. NARRATIVE ARC
What movement does this post run on? (contradiction reveal, escalation, scene-to-insight, confession-to-principle, diagnostic revelation, progressive build)

### 5. BODY STRUCTURE
How is the body organized? (single scene explored deeply, three examples building, before/after transformation, progressive revelation, numbered list with twist)

### 5b. BODY FORMAT (STRICT ENUM)
Pick the ONE body format that best describes the source's body structure. This is the structural mold a Batch A mirror MUST preserve.
- `numbered_list` — body is a numbered list (1. ..., 2. ..., 3. ...). Every numbered item is its own paragraph or block. The list IS the body.
- `bullet_list` — body is an unnumbered bulleted list (-, •, *, or similar) where each bullet is a discrete unit
- `parallel_paragraphs` — body has 2-4 parallel paragraphs with identical or near-identical structure (each opens with the same shape, builds the same way)
- `three_examples` — body presents three distinct examples building toward a conclusion. Not strictly numbered, but each example is clearly delimited.
- `before_after` — body presents a before/after pair (X was true; now Y is true) with the pivot as the engine.
- `progressive_revelation` — body progressively reveals an insight, each paragraph adding a new turn or layer.
- `single_scene` — body is one continuous scene or moment, explored deeply.
- `prose_essay` — body is flowing argumentative prose without clear discrete units; one paragraph leads to the next without parallel structure.

### 6b. CLOSER MECHANIC (STRICT ENUM)

### 10. OPENER QUALITY TESTS
Run these three tests on the source's opening line(s). These determine whether the opener is good enough to mirror (Batch A) or whether all posts should use different mechanics (Batch B only).

**MACRO test**: Does the opener land for someone OUTSIDE this niche? Read the opener to a stranger in a different industry. If they'd need domain context to understand what's being said — fail. Jargon, acronyms, category-specific terms ("AI-powered voice technology," "doctorless AI kiosks," "accessible communication") → fail. The opener must work universally.

Exception — audience-address openers: If the opener addresses a broad professional role that exists across multiple industries (e.g., "Founders:", "Sales leaders:", "CTOs:", "Operators:", "Engineers:", "Recruiters:", "Managers:"), this is NOT niche jargon. These pass macro because ANY professional recognizes the role even if they don't hold it. Fail only for subcategories that require domain knowledge to parse (e.g., "RevOps managers:", "MLOps engineers:", "Series B CFOs:").

**FAST test**: Does the specific, interesting, or dramatic word land in the FIRST 8 words? Count from word 1. If the payload (the specific number, the surprising claim, the tension) comes after word 8 — fail. Ellipsis that delays the punch ("The future of X is arriving...") → fail. "We lost $2M in three months" → pass (payload at word 3). "The future of healthcare is arriving... China unveils..." → fail (payload at word 11).

**SIMPLE test**: Would a 16-year-old understand every word in the opener? Bold Unicode formatting, corporate jargon ("unveils," "redefining," "accessible communication"), press-release language → fail. The opener must use words a person would speak in conversation, not words a company would put in a press release.

**Rule**: If ANY of the three tests fails, set `skip_batch_a` to true. The source's STRUCTURE may be mirrorable, but if its OPENER is a press release, corporate announcement, or jargon-heavy statement, mirroring it produces a press-release post. Route all posts to Batch B instead, where different mechanics will produce better openers.
