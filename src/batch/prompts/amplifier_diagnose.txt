## 7-GATE DIAGNOSIS

Run the gates in order. Each gate is cheap to run on a draft that already passed the previous one.

### Gate 1: MECHANIC (does the opener stop the scroll?)
Identify which of the 8 proven mechanics is being used:
1. Specific number + context — "$412K closed last quarter. Target was $350K."
2. Juxtaposition — "Our competitor raised $40M. We outshipped them."
3. First-person confession — "I told 50 buyers we were ready. I was wrong."
4. Quote hook + reaction — "'We need you to present to the supervisor.' That's when I knew the deal was dead."
5. Scene-entry — "We walked into their call center. Post-it notes on every monitor."
6. Decision reveal — "A Fortune 500 asked us to build a CRM. We said no."
7. Contrarian declaration — "Most founder advice is wrong."
8. Pattern observation — "Every founder I know who's crossed $20M runs into the same wall."

FAIL: Belief statements ("X matters"), question openers, "excited to announce," hedged setups, "I've been thinking about...", "What's your X strategy?"

### Gate 2: PLAUSIBILITY (would a real person actually say this in real life?)
Run this test on every opener containing a quote, scene, or stated dialogue. Specific failure shapes to catch:

- **Strawman quote**: A character delivering a too-convenient line that exists only so the post can knock it down. Real executives don't say these teed-up sentences at dinner.
- **Counted-precision tic**: "She has been in eleven AI strategy meetings since January. Eleven." Real people don't volunteer specific meeting counts. The repetition for emphasis is a written rhetorical move, not spoken.
- **Convenient setup quote**: Someone brags about something then can't answer the obvious follow-up. The exchange exists for the punchline.
- **The obvious-question quote from someone who wouldn't ask it**: Board members at Series B AI companies ask about pipeline, churn, runway — not "when does AI just handle everything?"
- **The impossible scenario**: The setup is logically impossible given the rest of the post.

If the opener has a quote or scene with another person, ask: would a real person in that role, in that moment, actually say or do this? If "no, this is set up too neatly to be real" — FAIL.

### Gate 3: COHERENCE (does the opener match the post's actual argument?)
The single most important test. An opener can be punchy AND wrong for the post. Two sub-tests, both required:

**Promise test**: Write down what the opener promises the reader, in one sentence. Read the body. Does every paragraph deliver against that promise, or does the body argue an adjacent point?

**Compression test**: Compress the body to one sentence. Compare to the opener. Same argument from different angles, or two different arguments?

Failure modes:
- **Drift** — opener is right, body wandered. Fix the body.
- **Bait-and-switch** — opener is sharper than the body delivers. Match one to the other.
- **Two posts in one** — opener for argument A, body for argument B. Split.

The opener has to be both universal (simple words, stakes a stranger gets) AND faithful to the actual argument. Macro-but-misleading damages the founder over time.

### Gate 4: SPECIFICITY (no invented facts)
Three degrees of specificity:
- **First-degree**: "Anant told me on the flight to Manila…" — Allowed ONLY if it's in the draft or the founder has publicly told this story.
- **Second-degree**: "A founder I back at a $40M company told me…" — Allowed only if the founder really has companies at that scale.
- **Third-degree**: "Every founder I know who's crossed $20M runs into the same thing…" — Always allowed.

Default to third-degree when in doubt. A pattern observation lands almost as hard as a named story and carries zero fabrication risk. Never invent first-degree.

### Gate 5: MODE PRESERVATION
The post arrives already marked as wrestling-mode or declaring-mode.

**Declaring-mode**: strongest opener is usually a clean assertion, contrarian declaration, or specific number. Body defends the assertion in a straight line.

**Wrestling-mode**: strongest opener signals movement is coming — a confession ("I told 50 buyers we were ready. I was wrong"), a contradiction ("Our biggest churn last quarter wasn't who I expected"), or a stated tension.

A wrestling-mode post with a clean declarative opener creates a bait-and-switch: reader is promised certainty, body delivers uncertainty. Don't silently strip wrestling structure to make an opener punchier. The wrestling IS the point.

### Gate 6: VOICE FIT (does this opener sound like THIS founder?)
Compare the opener against the founder's voice markers.

Two sub-tests:

1. **Recognition test**: Would this founder read the opener and think "this is what I was going to say"? Or would they think "this is what my marketing team wrote"? If the opener could appear in a corporate press release, product announcement, or generic LinkedIn post — FAIL. Test: strip the name and ask "who wrote this?" If the answer is "any executive" rather than "this specific founder" — FAIL.

2. **Marker test**: Does the opener contain or reflect at least 1 of the founder's voice markers? Not forced inclusion — natural expression. If the opener uses corporate register (e.g., "unveils," "AI-powered," "accessible communication," "the future of X is arriving," "redefining") instead of the founder's register — FAIL.

The voice markers are the law. If the opener is mechanically perfect (passes Gates 1-5) but sounds like someone else wrote it — FAIL Gate 6. A technically correct opener that doesn't sound like the founder is worse than a rough opener that does.

### Gate 7: SOURCE MIRROR FIDELITY (Batch A only)
Skip this gate entirely if source dissection is "N/A" (Batch B posts). Only Batch A mirrored posts are tested here.

Source primary mechanic: {source_hook_mechanic}
Source body format: {source_body_format}
Source closer mechanic: {source_closer_mechanic}
Source dissection: {source_dissection}

This gate has THREE independent sub-checks. ANY of them failing makes the whole gate FAIL. Do not give a charitable pass — the entire point of Batch A is that the generated post mirrors the source's MECHANIC, BODY FORMAT, and CLOSER. If any one is missing, the post is not a Batch A mirror.

**Sub-check A — OPENER MECHANIC (FAIL if mismatch)**
1. **Mechanic match**: Does the opener use the SAME primary mechanic as the source ({source_hook_mechanic})? If the opener substitutes a different mechanic (e.g., source uses "specific_number" but opener uses "contrarian") — FAIL.
2. **Sentence count match**: Does the opener have the same number of sentences as the source's hook?
3. **Word count proximity**: Is the opener's word count within ±20% of the source opener's word count?

**Sub-check B — BODY STRUCTURAL FORMAT (FAIL if mismatch)**
The source's body uses format: `{source_body_format}`. The generated post's body MUST use the SAME format.
- If source = `numbered_list` → generated post body MUST be a numbered list with discrete items (roughly the same item count, ±2). A flowing essay that mentions the same content as a list — FAIL.
- If source = `bullet_list` → generated post body MUST be a bulleted list.
- If source = `parallel_paragraphs` → generated post body MUST have parallel paragraph structure (each paragraph opening with the same shape).
- If source = `three_examples` → generated post body MUST present three distinct examples.
- If source = `before_after` → generated post body MUST have a before/after pivot.
- If source = `progressive_revelation` → flowing build allowed.
- If source = `single_scene` → one continuous scene required.
- If source = `prose_essay` → flowing argumentative prose required.

Inspect the GENERATED post's body. Identify its actual format. If it doesn't match the source's format → FAIL with `body_format_match=false` and name the actual format used. The most common failure: source is `numbered_list` but generated is `prose_essay` — this is a HARD FAIL.

**Sub-check C — CLOSER MECHANIC (FAIL if mismatch)**
The source closes with: `{source_closer_mechanic}`. The generated post's closer MUST use the SAME mechanic.
- `cta` ↔ `cta`: if source closes with a CTA ("I put together the breakdown," "link in comments"), the generated post must close with a parallel CTA.
- `reframe_question` ↔ `reframe_question`: if source closes with a reframe question, generated must close with a question.
- `terminal_verdict` ↔ `terminal_verdict`: matching declarative final line.
- And so on for `physical_image`, `challenge`, `callback`, `unresolved_tension`, `quiet_admission`.

Inspect the GENERATED post's closer. If the source uses `cta` and the generated uses `reframe_question` → FAIL with `closer_mechanic_match=false`.

**HOW TO FAIL GATE 7**: If ANY of sub-check A (mechanic), sub-check B (body format), or sub-check C (closer mechanic) fails, set `gates.source_mirror.pass = false`. Do NOT pass the gate just because most sub-checks pass — a Batch A post that mirrors the opener but reverts the body to prose has failed its core job.

The most common gate-7 failure to catch: source is a numbered-list listicle with a CTA closer; generated post is a flowing contrarian essay with a reframe-question closer. Three sub-checks failing. Pass=false. Be honest.

## BODY SCAN
Read the body line by line. For each line, ask:
- Could this sentence work as the opener, lifted out of where it currently sits?
- Does it have a number, a juxtaposition, a confession, a quote, or a scene?
- Is it punchier than what's currently in line 1?

If yes, mark it as buried gold. Don't draft the new opener until you've checked whether the post already contains its real opener three paragraphs in.
