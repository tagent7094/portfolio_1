## YOUR TASK

Decide whether this pack is shippable or whether one more transpose-regen pass would meaningfully improve it.

### SHIP CRITERIA — emit `pack_decision = "ship"` when ALL hold:
- ≥ 7 of 9 posts pass the 9/7 floor (per `passes_9_7_floor` field in validated_pack).
- No post has the register/posture flag (register_score < 7 OR posture_score < 7).
- Anchor diversity: at least 5 distinct `anchor_consumed_id` values across the pack.
- No two posts share the same argument_compressed thesis.
- Mechanic distribution: at least 4 distinct opener mechanics across Batch B.
- No fabricated specifics (anchor_grounding ≥ 7 on every Batch A post).
- No duplicate paragraphs within any post.

### REGEN_MORE CRITERIA — emit `pack_decision = "regen_more"` when ANY hold:
- More than 2 posts fail the 9/7 floor.
- Same anchor used 3+ times across pack.
- 2+ posts argue the same thesis.
- < 4 distinct mechanics across Batch B.
- Any Batch A post has fabricated first-person specifics.
- Any post has duplicate paragraphs.

### Special rule: REGEN HISTORY ceiling
- If `REGEN HISTORY` length ≥ 2 (this is the 3rd compile attempt), ALWAYS emit `pack_decision = "ship"` regardless of remaining issues. Set `compile_regen_ceiling_reached = true` in audit_summary and surface the residual issues in `failure_summary`. The operator will see the warning and manually rerun if needed. The system never blocks on an infinite improvement loop.

Critical rules:
- `pack_decision` is exactly one of: `"ship"` or `"regen_more"`. NEVER emit `"reject"`.
- When `REGEN HISTORY` has ≥ 2 entries, force `pack_decision = "ship"` and set `compile_regen_ceiling_reached = true`.
- `regen_instructions[]` MUST be populated when `pack_decision = "regen_more"`. Each entry needs `label`, `issue`, `instruction`.
- `instruction` should be specific enough that the transpose model can act on it without guessing what to change.
