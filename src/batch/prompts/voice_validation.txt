## SCORING

Score each dimension 0-5:

### 1. VOICE-MARKER COMPLIANCE
How many of the founder's voice markers appear naturally in the post?
- 0 = none of the markers are present
- 1 = 1 marker, forced or shallow
- 2 = 1 marker, well-integrated
- 3 = 2 markers, naturally expressed
- 4 = 3 markers, naturally expressed
- 5 = 4+ markers, seamlessly integrated

### 2. OPENER-RHYTHM MATCH
Does the opening follow the founder's documented argument rhythm pattern?
- 0 = completely different rhythm (e.g., corporate announcement vs. paradoxical declaration)
- 3 = partially matches (right shape, wrong register)
- 5 = matches the documented rhythm exactly

### 3. FORMATTING-HABIT MATCH
Does the post match the founder's formatting (em dashes, paragraph length, sentence structure, closing moves)?
- 0 = completely different formatting (e.g., bullet lists when founder uses flowing paragraphs)
- 3 = some formatting matches
- 5 = formatting is indistinguishable from the founder's published posts

### 4. REGISTER CHECK
Does the post read like the FOUNDER wrote it, or like someone else?
- 0 = reads like corporate copy, press release, or marketing statement
- 1 = reads like a generic thought leader
- 3 = reads like a founder in this space, but not THIS specific founder
- 5 = reads like the calibration paragraph's register — same voice, same posture

### 5. POSTURE CHECK (founder inside vs. outside)
Is the founder positioned as an OPERATOR inside the experience, or a COMMENTATOR watching from outside?
- 0 = pure commentator — the founder analyzes a category or trend as an outsider ("The industry is moving toward...", "BPO executives are realizing...")
- 2 = mixed — some first-person operational language but mostly third-person market commentary
- 3 = operator with distance — first-person but the experience described is generic, not specific to this founder's company
- 5 = fully inside — the founder speaks from lived operational experience ("We have a million agents on Sanas...", "When I was 19 in that call center...")

The founder's framework rule: "Put the CEO inside the experience — vulnerable, exposed, not watching from outside." A post where the founder comments on an industry trend like a market analyst FAILS posture even if the voice markers are right.

If register_score is below 3 → ALWAYS return "FAIL" regardless of other scores. A post that doesn't sound like the founder is the single most important failure to catch.
If posture_score is below 3 → return "FAIL". A founder who sounds like a market commentator instead of an operator is a voice failure.
